Impact on the Economic Activity of Germany

- The German GDP is expected to drop by between 9% and 12% in Q2 2020. For the entire year of 2020, an expected drop between 4% and 9% in a V or U shape recovery respectively.

- An estimated weekly loss of EUR 15bn or a 26% drop in the GDP is also expected.

- Reason for these drops arise from the weak demand in the country. Low demand resulting from consumer uncertainty, low consumer sentiment, and sectoral shut-downs, e.g. accommodation and food, air transport and parts of retail.

- Manufacturing activity: Was hit particularly hard with weekly GDP likely to drop by 38%, equivalent to EUR 5.2 bn.

- Retail sector: Strong growth in grocery and in online services while a dip in sales of apparel/ luxury or electronics/ IT store retailers.

Impact on Unemployment

- Compared with a year ago, the unemployment rate fell in 19 Member States, while remaining stable in Germany, about 3.3%.

-Similarly, the youth (under 25) unemployment rate (5.3%) in Germany was recorded to be the lowest in Europe.

- Though at its [Covid-19] peak, the unemployment rate will jump to 5.9% this year, and the ranks of short-time workers will swell to 2.5 million year over year.

- The unaffected unemployment rate possibly relates to the less-harsh lockdown protocol implemented in the country. Unlike its neighbors, Germany did not order its 80 million citizens to stay at home.

- Most shops remained open with social distancing protocols in place. Though there were a number of shops that were deemed non-essential which were forced to close down.
